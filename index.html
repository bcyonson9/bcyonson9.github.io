The Heart of Valencia: The Town Plaza

The Valencia Town Plaza, anchored by the historic St. Nicholas of Tolentine Parish Church, serves as the vibrant center of the municipality. It's the perfect place to witness local life, enjoy the cool mountain breeze, and appreciate the classic Filipino town structure.

Welcome to the Cool Highlands

Nestled just a short drive from Dumaguete City, Valencia offers a refreshing escape into nature. Known for its cool mountain climate, lush landscapes, geothermal features, and dramatic waterfalls, this municipality is a haven for adventurers and those seeking tranquility. Discover a town rich in history and teeming with natural wonders, thanks to its proximity to Mount Talinis.

Must-See Natural Wonders

Casaroro Falls

One of the most famous waterfalls in Negros Oriental. Reaching it involves a challenging hike and descent via a concrete stairway, but the sight of the majestic, narrow column of water plunging into a deep pool is an incredible reward.

Pulangbato Hot Springs

Experience the unique geothermal activity of Valencia. The 'Pulangbato' (red rock) area features sulfurous hot springs and a waterfall whose rocks are stained red and orange by mineral deposits. Perfect for a relaxing, therapeutic dip.

Fil-Am Japanese Shrine

A poignant historical site dedicated to the memory of the soldiers who perished during World War II in the mountains of Valencia. It offers a solemn atmosphere and sweeping panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Planning Your Adventure

Getting There

Valencia is easily accessible from Dumaguete City (about 30-45 minutes). Take a habal-habal (motorcycle taxi) or hire a multi-cab for a group.

Best Time to Visit

The best time is during the dry season (December to May) for easier trekking, though the cool climate is enjoyable year-round.

Local Flavors

Don't miss the local coffee plantations! The area is known for producing high-quality arabica beans due to its high altitude and rich volcanic soil.
